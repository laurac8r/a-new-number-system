I love this prompt. Designing a number system from scratch is a fantastic exercise in logic and creativity.

Let me introduce you to **The Hexaflora System**.

Instead of being based on our 10 fingers, Hexaflora is a **Base-6** system inspired by botany and the growth of a plant. Mathematically, Base-6 (also known as senary) is incredibly efficient because 6 is cleanly divisible by both 2 and 3, making fractions much easier to deal with than in our standard Base-10 system.

Here is how it works.

### 1. The Digits (0 through 5)

Because it is a Base-6 system, we only need six distinct symbols. In Hexaflora, the symbols represent the stages of a plant growing from a seed into a bloom.

| Decimal Value | Hexaflora Symbol | Stage Name | Visual Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **0** | **⚬** | Seed | An unsprouted, round seed. |
| **1** | **⚲** | Shoot | A single stem pushing up. |
| **2** | **♈︎** | Split | Two leaves branching out. |
| **3** | **☘** | Clover | Three distinct leaves. |
| **4** | **✤** | Bud | A four-pointed flowering bud. |
| **5** | **❀** | Bloom | A full five-petaled flower. |

---

### 2. Place Values

Just like our normal numbers, Hexaflora is a positional system. But instead of the columns being worth 1s, 10s, 100s, and 1000s, they multiply by 6 each time you move left.

* **Column 1 (Rightmost):** The 1s place
* **Column 2:** The 6s place
* **Column 3:** The 36s place
* **Column 4:** The 216s place

### 3. How to Count

When you reach the number 6 in the real world, you run out of single Hexaflora symbols. So, just like reaching 10 in our system, you move over one column to the left.

Here is how you count from zero to twelve:

* **0:** ⚬
* **1:** ⚲
* **2:** ♈︎
* **3:** ☘
* **4:** ✤
* **5:** ❀
* **6:** ⚲⚬ *(One 6, zero 1s)*
* **7:** ⚲⚲ *(One 6, one 1)*
* **8:** ⚲♈︎ *(One 6, two 1s)*
* **9:** ⚲☘ *(One 6, three 1s)*
* **10:** ⚲✤ *(One 6, four 1s)*
* **11:** ⚲❀ *(One 6, five 1s)*
* **12:** ♈︎⚬ *(Two 6s, zero 1s)*

---

### 4. Reading a Large Number

Let's look at a larger number written in Hexaflora: **❀⚲✤**

To translate this back to Base-10, we break it down by its place value columns:

* The left **❀** is in the 36s place. So, 5 × 36 = **180**
* The middle **⚲** is in the 6s place. So, 1 × 6 = **6**
* The right **✤** is in the 1s place. So, 4 × 1 = **4**

Add them all together (180 + 6 + 4), and **❀⚲✤** equals **190** in our standard numbers!
